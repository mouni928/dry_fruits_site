Apricot

Apricot is a sweet, juicy fruit with a smooth or fuzzy skin, belonging to the Prunus genus, often enjoyed fresh or dried.

Bhadham

Almonds, commonly known as "badam," are packed with nutrients such as healthy fats, fiber, vitamins, and minerals, promoting heart health and aiding in weight management. Regular consumption may also support brain function and contribute to overall well-being.

Cashew

Cashew is a kidney-shaped nut commonly used in cooking and snacking, known for its creamy texture and mild, buttery flavor.It is also a key ingredient in vegan and dairy-free recipes for creating creamy sauces and desserts.

Dates

Dates are sweet, chewy fruits grown on date palm trees, rich in nutrients like fiber, potassium, and vitamins. They are often consumed as a natural sweetener or eaten on their own as a healthy snack.

Walnut

Walnuts are edible seeds from the walnut tree, known for their distinct brain-like appearance, and they have a rich, slightly bitter taste. They are a good source of omega-3 fatty acids, antioxidants, and vitamins, offering several health benefits.

Raisin

Raisins are dried grapes, known for their sweet taste and wrinkled texture, often used in baking,cooking, and as a nutritious addition to cereals or snacks. They are a concentrated source of natural sugars, fiber, and antioxidants.

Kismis

Kismis is the term used for raisins in South Asia, made by drying grapes, and commonly used in desserts, snacks, and various dishes for their sweet flavor and texture. They are a good source of energy and essential nutrients.

Pista

Pista, also known as pistachio, is a greenish nut with a slightly sweet and nutty flavor, commonly used in both sweet and savory dishes and enjoyed as a nutritious snack. They are rich in healthy fats, protein, and various essential nutrients.
